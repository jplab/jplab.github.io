<p><strong>Research interests</strong></p>

<p>My speciality lies in unifying computational, combinatorial, and geometric methods to create novel frameworks to study structures in algebra, geometry, and quantum physics.
In particular, my research merges large-scale computational experimentations and abstract modelizations to tackle open problems related to Coxeter and reflection groups, polytopes (in particular the permutahedra, associahedra and their generalizations), triangulations, and simplicial complexes.</p>

<p>In general, I appreciate particularly problems related to the following themes:</p>
<ul class="simple">
<li>Algebra: representation theory, Coxeter groups, infinite root systems, Lie groups, cluster algebras</li>
<li>Combinatorics: counting problems, combinatorics on words, lattice theory, relation to algebraic structures</li>
<li>Convex Geometry: triangulations, realization spaces of polytopes, permutahedra, associahedra and their generalizations</li>
<li>Computational geometry and algebra: generation problems, isomorphism problems, complexity, visualization tools</li>
</ul>
<p>Although not an expert, I appreciate to hear about</p>
<ul class="simple">
<li>Quantum physics</li>
<li>Computational complexity, imagery, tilings, fractals</li>
<li>Toric varieties and lattice polytopes</li>
<li>Tropical geometry</li>
<li>Hyperbolic geometry</li>
</ul>
